Enhancing Independence for Individuals with Visual
Impairments through Computer Vision

Introduce

Individuals with visual problems have a hard time navigating their surroundings. The need for assistance in the reliance on guide dogs, canes, or other individuals is crucial in their daily lives. Despite the availability of guide dogs and canes, individuals with visual impairments often encounter difficulties in unfamiliar environments. These tools do not provide real-time information about obstacles, changes in terrain, or dynamic elements such as moving vehicles and people. This lack of real-time situational awareness can lead to accidents, reduced mobility, and a lower quality of life. However, a particular advancement in the field of technologies called computer vision has promising potential to help people with visual problems.

Computer vision is a part of artificial intelligence to scan the environment and interpret the information from the visual scan. There's been a lot of advancement in the field of computer vision over the years. By leveraging the potential usage of computer vision to analyze, identify, and describe their surroundings objects, environment, and activities in real-time, this technology holds a lot of usability in assisting visual impairment peoples to know their surroundings in real time in a way that was previously much harder to do for them. This mini research hopes to create a better understanding of computer visions and its advancing field in assisting and creating better independence for visual impairment peoples. The limitation of this mini research is there need to be a more realistic application by using the real technologies mentioned in its literature reviews.

Significance



This research has the potential to make major contributions to assistive technology for individuals with visual impairments, increasing their independence by providing real-time information about their surroundings.



Computer vision technology can reduce reliance on guide dogs, canes, or human assistance, and improve quality of life by increasing situational awareness and safety.



By analyzing recent innovations in computer vision, this research can strengthen support for further development in the field of assistive technology and increase public awareness of its benefits.

Theoritical

Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that enables computers to interpret and make decisions based on visual data. It involves techniques for acquiring, processing, and analyzing images to generate numerical or symbolic information. Key components of computer vision include image recognition, object detection, and scene reconstruction, which are essential for identifying objects, locating them in images or video frames, and creating 3D models from 2D images. Artificial intelligence, particularly machine learning and deep learning, plays a crucial role in computer vision by training models to recognize patterns and make decisions from visual inputs, with deep learning using neural networks to analyze complex data patterns.

Method

Data Collection

This study will collect data through a literature review of scholarly publications on computer vision, AI, and assistive technology. Sources in the medical field from the internet will be explored to gather relevant and diverse literature.

Data Analysis

Thematic analysis will be used to identify trends and innovations in applying computer vision for visual impairments.

Synthesis of Findings

The findings will be synthesized through comparative analysis, highlighting similarities, differences, and research gaps. A summary of impactful innovations will be provided, along with recommendations for future research and practical applications to enhance assistive technology.

Finding

Finding

The WorldScribe system demonstrates that the technology provides adaptive, contextualized real-time visual descriptions to help individuals with visual impairments understand their environment. WorldScribe allows users to customize the type of information received through an interface that is responsive to both voice and visual input, and can manage social interactions by pausing descriptions when a conversation is in progress and increasing the volume when important sounds are present. While offering many benefits in increasing user independence and participation, challenges remain in conveying complex visual information in a way that does not impose a cognitive burden on the user, indicating the need for a more humanistic and user-focused approach in the development of this technology.

Computer vision is a key technology in artificial intelligence, dedicated to processing and analyzing images to extract meaningful insights. By leveraging advanced algorithms rooted in machine learning (ML), deep learning (DL), and artificial neural networks (ANN), computer vision systems can perform complex tasks such as object recognition, image segmentation, and scene understanding. These capabilities allow machines to interpret visual data with a level of accuracy and efficiency that mimics human vision.

Healthcare is one of the most impactful domains for computer vision applications, driving advancements in medical diagnostics and treatment planning. Recently, computer vision has been widely utilized in analyzing medical images such as X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans. This technology not only assists in identifying abnormalities with higher accuracy but also supports healthcare professionals in making faster, data-driven decisions. Furthermore, computer vision plays a crucial role in early disease detection, surgical assistance, and patient monitoring, contributing to overall improved healthcare outcomes.

Discussion

Assistive Technologies

Computer vision in assistive technologies, like WorldScribe, enhances the independence of individuals with visual impairments by providing real-time, adaptive descriptions of their surroundings. However, a key challenge is balancing the amount of information delivered to avoid overwhelming users' cognitive capacity, requiring a user-centered, simple design approach.

Healthcare:

In healthcare, computer vision improves diagnostic accuracy and speed by analyzing medical images such as X-rays and MRIs. Machine learning and deep learning enable more precise detection of abnormalities, supporting early disease detection and surgical precision, which accelerates decision-making for healthcare professionals.

Challenges and Solutions

Both assistive technologies and healthcare face challenges, including avoiding cognitive overload in assistive systems and ensuring algorithm fairness and accuracy in healthcare. Addressing these challenges requires a multidisciplinary approach, focusing on inclusive data collection, ethical considerations, and transparent AI systems to maximize the impact of computer vision.
